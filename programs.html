The Elite Scholar Program is our flagship initiative, identifying and supporting the most exceptional young minds globally. Scholars receive comprehensive financial support for their education, access to our global network of partner institutions, and personalized development plans to maximize their potential.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Aged 14-22 at time of application
  • Demonstrated exceptional achievement in at least one domain (academics, innovation, leadership, arts, athletics)
  • Strong academic record (typically top 1% in standardized assessments)
  • Evidence of original thinking and problem-solving abilities
  • Commitment to creating positive impact

Selection Process

1

Application Submission

Submit detailed application with academic records, achievements, and personal statements.

2

Initial Screening

Applications are reviewed by our selection committee against rigorous criteria.

3

Assessment Challenges

Shortlisted candidates complete a series of innovative assessment challenges.

4

Interview Rounds

Multiple interview rounds with academic experts, industry leaders, and foundation directors.

5

Final Selection

Holistic evaluation of candidate potential, achievements, and alignment with foundation values.

Specialized Scholarship Programs

STEM Excellence

For exceptional talents in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics

Provides specialized support for advanced research opportunities, access to cutting-edge laboratories, and mentorship from leading scientists and researchers.

Special Features: Research grants, conference sponsorship, lab access at partner institutions

Creative Visionaries

For extraordinary talents in arts, design, and creative innovation

Supports the development of creative excellence through specialized training, exhibition opportunities, studio space, and collaboration with established artists and designers.

Special Features: Portfolio development, exhibition sponsorship, creative residencies

Social Impact Innovators

For young leaders driving positive social change

Empowers scholars working on innovative solutions to pressing social and environmental challenges with dedicated mentorship, seed funding, and impact assessment support.

Special Features: Social venture funding, impact measurement tools, policy advocacy training

GEF Mentorship Network

Our comprehensive mentorship program connects scholars with world-class experts across diverse fields. Each mentor relationship is carefully cultivated to provide guidance, industry insights, and professional development opportunities.

Academic Mentors

Leading professors, researchers, and academic directors from prestigious institutions worldwide who provide guidance on research methodology, academic publication, and specialized knowledge development.

Industry Leaders

Executives, entrepreneurs, and innovators from global companies and startups who share real-world insights, industry trends, and career development strategies.

Creative Professionals

Accomplished artists, writers, designers, and creative directors who nurture creative expression, portfolio development, and artistic career pathways.

Policy Experts

Government advisors, diplomats, and policy specialists who provide perspectives on public service, policy development, and international relations.

Mentorship Structure

Each scholar is matched with a primary mentor and has access to a wider network of specialized advisors. The mentorship structure includes:

  • Monthly one-on-one sessions with primary mentor
  • Quarterly group mentorship seminars
  • Annual mentorship retreat
  • On-demand access to specialized advisors
  • Collaborative projects with mentor guidance

Innovation & Entrepreneurship Initiatives

Innovation Grant Program

Seed funding for promising research and entrepreneurial ventures

Provides grants ranging from $5,000 to $25,000 to support innovative projects with potential for significant impact. Includes mentorship and structured development support.

Recent Success: QuantEdge Solutions, founded by scholar Yusei Fujii, received initial funding through this program and has since raised $1.2M in additional investment.

Venture Accelerator

Comprehensive support for high-potential startups

Intensive 6-month program providing equity-free funding up to $100,000, office space, legal support, and connections to industry partners and investors.

Program Features: Weekly masterclasses, pitch events, investor introductions, and personalized mentorship from successful entrepreneurs.

Patent Support Initiative

Intellectual property protection and commercialization assistance

Provides legal guidance, patent application support, and commercialization strategy for scholars' innovations. Covers costs associated with patent filing and protection.

Success Metric: 43 patents filed by scholars in the past year, with 12 already granted and 5 successfully licensed to industry partners.

Leadership Development Programs

Our leadership initiatives are designed to nurture the next generation of global leaders who combine exceptional capability with ethical purpose, strategic vision, and cross-cultural competence.

Annual gathering of scholars, alumni, and global leaders for intensive leadership training, networking, and collaborative problem-solving.

  • Cross-cultural negotiation simulations

Governance & Ethics Program

Specialized training in ethical leadership, governance, and responsible decision-making across private, public, and non-profit sectors.

Public Service Leadership Track

  • Public sector innovation training

Global Exchange Programs

Our exchange initiatives create opportunities for scholars to gain international experience, develop cross-cultural competencies, and build global networks while pursuing their academic and professional goals.

International Service Projects

Collaborative initiatives addressing global challenges through on-the-ground service and innovation. Scholars work with local communities and organizations on sustainable solutions.

Recent Projects: Clean water technologies in rural India, Educational technology implementation in Kenya, Climate resilience planning in Pacific island nations
