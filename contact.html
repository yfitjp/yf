Frequently Asked Questions

How can I apply to become a GEF scholar?

Scholar applications are accepted annually between September 1 and December 15. Eligible candidates must be between 14-22 years old and demonstrate exceptional achievement in at least one area of focus (academics, innovation, leadership, arts, or athletics). Visit our Programs page for detailed application requirements and to access the online application portal.

Does GEF offer funding for research or entrepreneurial projects?

Yes, GEF provides several funding opportunities for both scholars and alumni. Our Innovation Grant program offers seed funding of $5,000-$25,000 for promising research initiatives and startup ventures. Additionally, our Venture Accelerator program provides more substantial funding and mentorship for select entrepreneurial projects with significant potential impact.

How can educational institutions partner with GEF?

We collaborate with universities, research institutions, and secondary schools worldwide through our Educational Partnership Network. Partner institutions may host GEF scholars, provide specialized mentorship, offer advanced educational opportunities, or collaborate on research initiatives. Please contact partners@globalexcellence.org to discuss potential collaboration opportunities.

Does GEF offer programs for students who don't qualify for full scholarships?

Yes, beyond our core scholarship program, we offer the GEF Mentorship Network, Summer Institutes, and online MasterClass series that are available to promising students who may not meet the full criteria for our Elite Scholar designation. These programs provide valuable guidance, skill development, and networking opportunities to a broader pool of talented young individuals.
